Agent-based model toy demo: shock, dilution, and clustering

This visualization explains an agent-based model as a small artificial theatre world. Performers are agents. Each year, newcomers enter, productions are cast, and inactive performers retire. Casting is partly preferential: performers who already share network ties are more likely to be cast together. In the shock year, funding increases, creating more productions and more newcomers. The question is whether dilution alone can reproduce a drop in average clustering.
At the shock year, productions and newcomer influx permanently increase.
