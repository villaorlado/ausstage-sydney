Chao1 intuition: estimating missing nodes from singletons and doubletons

A toy visualization for explaining how a sampling process can suggest that some network nodes are probably missing. Here “species” are network nodes/performers, and “detections” are sampled appearances in records. Chao1 uses the number of nodes seen once and twice to estimate how many additional nodes remain unseen.

Interpretation: the Chao1 score is the estimated total number of nodes in the underlying population. The estimated captured percentage is observed nodes divided by Chao1. Many singletons and few doubletons suggest a long tail of nodes that have not yet appeared in the sample. Many doubletons suggest that the rare tail is becoming repeatedly observed, so the unseen estimate drops.
